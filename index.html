Exploratory Data Analysis using python libraries

Project brief:

The EDA performed on Diwali sale analysis is the process of exploring, analyzing and visualizing the Diwali sale data to gain insights into customer behavior and purchase patterns. The analysis was performed using Python, and the pandas, numpy, matplotlib and seaborn libraries were used for data manipulation, cleaning and visualization.
The EDA process involved the following steps:
1. Data loading and cleaning: The Diwali sale data was loaded into a pandas dataframe and cleaned to remove any missing or duplicate values and to ensure that the data is in the correct format.
2. Data exploration: Descriptive statistics, data visualization and other techniques were used to explore the data and gain insights into customer behavior and purchase patterns. The data was analyzed by age, gender, product category, discount and purchase amount.
3. Data analysis: Statistical tests and machine learning algorithms were used to analyze the data and identify significant patterns and trends. The analysis showed that men tend to spend more than women, customers aged 30-40 spend the most, electronic products are the most popular product category, followed by fashion and home decor, and discounts of 20-30% are the most effective at driving sales.
4. Conclusion: The results of the EDA were summarized and conclusions were drawn regarding customer behavior and purchase patterns during the Diwali sale. These insights can be used to inform future sales and marketing strategies, such as targeted discounts for specific customer segments or product categories.

Overall, the EDA performed on Diwali sale analysis provides valuable insights into customer behavior and purchase patterns, which can be used to make informed business decisions and improve sales and marketing strategies..
